Youku Dual Subtitle

Language learning companion for Youku videos

Overview

Youku Dual Subtitle is a Chrome extension that enhances your Chinese language learning experience by displaying dual subtitles (Chinese + your chosen language) with interactive features.

Features

Dual Subtitle Display: Shows Chinese subtitles (Simplified or Traditional) alongside a second language of your choice for context.
Interactive Dictionary: Hover over any Chinese word to see its definition, pinyin, and tone colors. The video automatically pauses when you hover over the Chinese subtitle.
Tone Colors: Chinese characters and pinyin are color-coded by tone (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, neutral) to help with pronunciation. Choose from 5 different color schemes.
Automatic Pause: Enable this feature to automatically pause the video when a new subtitle appears, giving you time to study the sentence.
Subtitle Navigation: Use keyboard shortcuts to jump between subtitles, perfect for reviewing dialogues and skipping silent parts.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Subtitle Navigation

Key Action
A Jump to the previous subtitle
D Jump to the next subtitle
S Replay the current subtitle from the beginning
P Toggle Automatic Pause on/off
Space Play/Pause video (standard video player control)

Dictionary Lookups (when hovering over a word)

Key Action
Alt + 1 Look up word in LINE Dict
Alt + 2 Look up pronunciation on Forvo
Alt + 3 Look up word in Dict.cn
Alt + 4 Look up word in iCIBA
Alt + 5 Look up word in MDBG dictionary
Alt + 6 Look up word on Reverso
Alt + 7 Look up word in MoeDict
T Find example sentences on Tatoeba
G Look up grammar notes on AllSet Learning (if available)
V Look up vocabulary notes on AllSet Learning (if available)
Note: Keyboard shortcuts work anywhere on the page except when typing in input fields. Dictionary lookup shortcuts only work when hovering over a Chinese word and the popup is visible.

Settings

Chinese Variant

Choose between Simplified Chinese (简体中文) or Traditional Chinese (繁体中文). This affects both the subtitle display and dictionary lookups.

Second Language

Select a secondary subtitle language for translation context. Available languages depend on what Youku provides for each video. Common options include English, Spanish, Japanese, Korean, and others.

Tone Color Scheme

Choose your preferred color scheme for pinyin tones. Neutral tones are painted in Grey:

  • Pleco: Red, Green, Blue, Purple (default)
  • Dummit: Red, Orange, Green, Light Blue
  • Sinosplice: Orange, Green, Blue, Red
  • Hanping: Light Blue, Green, Orange, Red
  • MDBG: Red, Orange, Green, Blue

Font Size

Adjust the subtitle font size using the − and + buttons. Range: 10px to 40px.

Show Pinyin Above Characters

Toggle whether pinyin (pronunciation guide) appears above Chinese characters in the subtitle. Disable this for a cleaner look if you don't need pronunciation help.

Automatic Pause

When enabled, the video will automatically pause each time a new subtitle appears. This is extremely useful for language learners who want to study each sentence carefully before continuing.

How to Use

  1. Navigate to a Youku video: Go to https://www.youku.tv/v/v_show/...
  2. Wait for subtitles to load: The extension will automatically detect available subtitles.
  3. Open the popup: Click the extension icon in your Chrome toolbar.
  4. Configure settings: Choose your preferred Chinese variant, second language, and other options.
  5. Study the subtitles: Watch the video with dual subtitles displayed.
  6. Hover over words: Hover your mouse over any Chinese word to see its definition and pronunciation.
  7. Navigate efficiently: Use A, D, and S keys to jump between dialogues.

Tips for Language Learners

  • Enable Automatic Pause: This gives you time to read, understand, and look up words before the video continues.
  • Use subtitle navigation: Press D to skip silent parts and move directly to the next dialogue.
  • Replay for practice: Press S to repeat the current subtitle line for shadowing or pronunciation practice.
  • Hover for definitions: Take advantage of the built-in dictionary to learn new words in context.
  • Try different tone colors: Find the color scheme that helps you best distinguish tones.
  • Drag to reposition: Click and drag the subtitle overlay to move it to your preferred position on screen.

Privacy

This extension works completely offline once installed. The dictionary data is stored locally, and no information is sent to external servers. All settings are saved locally in your browser.

Troubleshooting

Subtitles not appearing?

  • Make sure you're on a Youku video page (youku.tv/v/v_show/...)
  • Wait a few seconds for the video player to fully load
  • Check that the video has subtitles available
  • Try reloading the page

Dictionary not working?

  • If you see "Extension context invalidated", reload the page (F5)
  • This happens when the extension is reloaded while the page is open

Subtitles in wrong position?

  • Click and drag the subtitle overlay to reposition it
  • Your preferred position will be saved automatically
